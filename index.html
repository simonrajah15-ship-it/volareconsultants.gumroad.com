Identifying peer-reviewed research as the gold standard — versus blogs, viral posts, and unsourced AI summaries. Game 2 — Source Cards: Which Source Would You Trust? Selecting the most reliable source from four options for vaccine safety research — recognising conflict of interest and missing accountability. Game 3 — MCQ: Spot the AI Hallucination Recognising that AI fabricated an entire named researcher, career, and national award. Confident specificity is not the same as accuracy. Game 4 — Sequencing: The Research Process in Order Arranging five research steps in correct sequence: question, find sources, evaluate, cross-check, conclude. Game 5 — Prompt Refiner: Pick the Best Research Prompt Selecting the prompt that specifies 3 models, one example each, and a comparison — rather than asking broadly about a topic. Game 6 — MCQ: Fact, Opinion, or Inference? Distinguishing a logical inference (drawn from evidence but not directly proven) from a fact or an opinion. Game 7 — Matching: Match Claim to Verification Method Connecting three claim types to their correct verification source: scientific claim, current event, historical fact. Game 8 — MCQ: Spot the Bias in the Source Identifying conflict of interest when a company publishes research about its own product — regardless of professional credentials. Game 9 — MCQ: Build the Right Conclusion Synthesising three studies with mixed findings into a nuanced, evidence-matched conclusion.
